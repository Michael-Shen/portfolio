Builder Overview

What I build, why, and the problems I choose to solve.

15+ Years in Validation, Now Building Products
Positioning

Decision support, not just apps

Every product I've shipped reduces a small, recurring decision under time pressure into one clear answer — eat this or not, say this next, do this task first, is this product safe. I build the layer between "too much information" and "what do I do right now."

Audience

Busy people, small daily decisions

Professionals in meetings (Outshine), people managing what they eat (EatOrNot AI), anyone triaging a long task list (Critical Choice), and Taiwan consumers checking if a product they bought was recalled (食安雷達). Different apps, same shape of user: someone who needs a fast, trustworthy answer, not another dashboard to read.

Execution

Ship fast, harden after

15+ years as a Primary Test Owner and validation engineer at IBM, Synopsys, Cypress, and Parade taught me to distrust anything that isn't tested and hardened before it reaches real users — that discipline now runs my solo build process. I use AI-assisted development (Claude Code) alongside Flutter and Firebase to move from prototype to production in days, not months. 食安雷達 is a real example: built, deployed, security-hardened, and launched with a custom domain and transactional email in a single week.

Proof

Three apps, one civic tool, all in production

EatOrNot AI, Outshine, and Critical Choice are live on the App Store; 食安雷達 is live in production as a public civic-data tool. Traction numbers aren't published yet — that's a next milestone, not a gap I'm hiding — but every product here is real, shipped, and in someone's hands today.

Build System

How I move from idea to shipped product.

Solo Founder, Production Discipline
Product

Discovery To Release

Start from a real, narrow problem (not a feature list). Build a working version fast with AI-assisted development, ship it to real infrastructure — App Store or production hosting — rather than a private demo, then harden security and cost controls before pointing real users at it.

Audience

Content Flywheel

Not built out yet. Currently focused on shipping product; distribution and public sharing (YouTube, write-ups) are the next layer once there's something worth showing.

Technology

Tools and Stack

Flutter for mobile apps; Firebase (Hosting, Firestore, Cloud Functions) for backend and web products; Resend for transactional email; Cloudflare for DNS; GitHub for version control; Claude Code for AI-assisted development end to end, from first prototype to production deploy.

Next

From Shipped To Proven

Four products are live; the current focus is turning that into a track record — gathering real usage and feedback on each app, tightening the ones with the most signal, and applying the same ship-fast-harden-after discipline to whatever problem I take on next.
